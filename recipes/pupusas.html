Pupusas

Photo by Chris Gold, via Flickr, licensed under CC BY-NC 2.0

Description

Pupusas are the national dish of El Salvador and one of the most beloved street foods in all of Central America. These thick, handmade corn tortillas are stuffed with a variety of fillings — most traditionally a combination of cheese, refried beans, and chicharrón — then pressed flat and cooked on a hot griddle until golden and slightly crispy on the outside while remaining soft and melty within.

Pupusas are traditionally served with curtido, a tangy and lightly fermented cabbage slaw, and a thin tomato salsa.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 3 cups masa harina
  • 2 ¾ cup water
  • 1 ½ cups Quesillo cheese (can substitute shredded mozzarella)
  • Vegetable oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the salt and masa harina. Add the water and knead the dough with your hands until it forms a Play-Doh-like consistency.
  2. Form the dough into 12 equal balls about 2 inches in diameter. Use your fingers to form a pocket in the middle of each ball, leaving the edges thick.
  3. Add about 2 tablespoons of shredded cheese into the pocket. Use the edges of the dough to cover and seal the pupusa closed.
  4. Wet your hands lightly with water or oil and gently flatten the pupusa into a disk.
  5. Heat a cast iron skillet over medium heat and brush lightly with vegetable oil.
  6. Cook the pupusa for about 3-5 minutes on one side until crispy and golden brown. Flip and cook the other side.
  7. Repeat, re-oiling the skillet between each batch.
  8. Serve with salsa and curtido and enjoy!
