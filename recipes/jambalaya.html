Jambalaya

“Jambalaya” by Mark Bonica, via Flickr, licensed under CC BY 2.0

Description

Jambalaya is a bold and hearty one-pot dish that sits at the heart of Louisiana's Cajun culinary tradition. Born from the bayous and backwoods of south Louisiana, Cajun jambalaya gets its deep, smoky flavor from browned meat and the holy trinity of Cajun cooking — onion, celery, and bell pepper — all cooked down together before the rice absorbs every last drop of flavor in the pot.

In New Orleans and across Louisiana, jambalaya is more than just a meal. It is a staple of backyard gatherings, festival food, and family tradition. One pot, one spoon, and enough to feed a crowd — that is the spirit of jambalaya.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons peanut oil, divided
  • 1 tablespoon cajun seasoning
  • 10 ounces andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breast cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 small green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 ribs celery, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (16 oz) can crushed italian tomatoes
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon hot pepper sauce
  • 2 teaspoons worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon filé powder
  • 1 1/4 cups uncooked white rice
  • 2 1/2 cups chicken broth

Instructions

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of peanut oil in a large heavy Dutch oven over medium heat. Season sausage and chicken pieces with Cajun seasoning. Sauté sausage until browned. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon peanut oil and sauté chicken pieces until lightly browned on all sides. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  4. In the same pot, sauté onion, bell pepper, celery, and garlic until tender.
  5. Stir in crushed tomatoes and season with red pepper, black pepper, salt, hot pepper sauce, Worcestershire sauce, and filé powder.
  6. Stir in the chicken and sausage. Cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Stir in the rice and chicken broth.
  8. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 20 to 25 minutes, or until liquid is absorbed.
  9. Serve and enjoy!
